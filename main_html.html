Internet Security Best Practices

Learn essential rules to protect yourself online and stay safe in the digital world

Password Security

Strong passwords are your first line of defense against cyber threats. A secure password should be unique, complex, and difficult to guess.

  • Use at least 12 characters combining uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Never reuse passwords across different accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible
  • Use a password manager to store credentials securely
  • Change passwords immediately if you suspect a breach
  • Avoid using personal information like birthdays or names

Recognizing Phishing Attempts

Phishing is a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity. Stay vigilant and recognize the warning signs.

  • Check sender email addresses carefully for subtle misspellings
  • Hover over links before clicking to verify the actual destination
  • Be suspicious of urgent requests for personal information
  • Look for grammar and spelling errors in official-looking emails
  • Verify requests through official channels before responding
  • Never provide sensitive data via email or unsecured websites

Safe Handling of Company Data

Protecting corporate information is crucial for business security and compliance. Follow these guidelines to handle sensitive data responsibly.

  • Only access company data on authorized and secured devices
  • Use encrypted connections when accessing data remotely
  • Never share confidential information via unsecured channels
  • Lock your computer when leaving your workstation
  • Report any suspected data breaches immediately
  • Follow your organization's data classification policies

Social Media Behavior Guidelines

Social networks can expose personal and professional information. Practice smart social media habits to protect your privacy and reputation.

  • Review and adjust privacy settings on all social media accounts
  • Think before posting - consider how information could be misused
  • Avoid sharing detailed location information in real-time
  • Be cautious about accepting friend requests from unknown people
  • Don't share sensitive company information on personal accounts
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each social media platform
